3. TWO SPEC-AFFECTING REFINEMENTS (architect please fold in)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
R1 — coaching_assignments UNIQUE = (team_id, user_id, role, scope), NOT
   (team_id,user_id[,active]). Consequences for the comp RPCs:
     • A coach can hold MULTIPLE comp rows on one team (different role/scope) —
       that's why 11 comp > 7 staff. The "rate" is whichever ACTIVE row the pay
       trigger picks (scope matches event_type, highest rate, tiebreak created_at).
     • assign_team_coach / set_coach_comp must upsert on the FULL composite
       (team,user,role,scope). Changing a coach's role or scope creates a NEW row,
       it does not update the old one — so the RPC must deactivate the prior
       active row (active=false) and insert/activate the new one, or it silently
       leaves two active rows and the trigger's "highest rate wins" picks one.
     • Staff-tab comp display: show the single ACTIVE row matching the trigger's
       lookup, not a raw count. One coach can look like 2 rates otherwise.
R2 — CoachRateSheet (the Financials-lane editor) ONLY EDITS existing
   coaching_assignments.pay_per_session_cents per active row (keyed by row id) +
   staff_profiles.default_payout_method. It does NOT create comp rows. The new
   Staff-tab "Edit comp" (set_coach_comp) would be a SECOND editor of the same
   field. Not a hard conflict (both write pay_per_session_cents), but two UIs for
   one value drifts over time. DECISION (architect): does the Staff-tab comp
   SUPERSEDE CoachRateSheet, or coexist? Lean: coexist short-term (identical
   writes), converge the Financials rate editor into the Staff tab later. Either
   way, both must write the same field with the same units (cents).

  Q "Will the new Teams build edit/assign the coach?"
   -> Yes — the PR-2 Staff tab: Assign coach, Change role, Remove, all admin-only.
  Q "The coach gets a rate — tie it into the package."
   -> Yes — the assign flow writes team_staff AND coaching_assignments together

      event_coach_assignments(per-event pay) -> coach_payouts(settles a batch).
      A coach can self-see their own pay (RLS), never family money or peers' rates.
