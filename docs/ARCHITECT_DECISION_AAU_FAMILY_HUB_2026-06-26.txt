B. THE CORRECTION — D-FV2 + §7.2 : PAYWALL ≠ CHILD-SAFETY GATE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CC's own §2/D-FV2 sub-lean had the right instinct ("OPEN for public tracking; GATED
before anything family-private"). But §7.2 then says the PAYWALL is that gate ("Q1
ANSWERED by 7.2"). That over-collapses two independent things:

  - PAYMENT proves someone has a card. It does NOT prove the child they're adding is
    theirs. A paid container CONTAINS the data; it does not VERIFY the adult↔child
    relationship — and because "contained" makes the data private, it's actually
    LESS auditable, not more.
  - Failure mode this enables: an adult pays (card passes), selects a team from the
    scraped inventory, pins jersey #5 to "their kid" (who isn't), uploads "film"
    containing that child, and receives that child's where-and-when. The paywall
    stopped none of it.

So Q1 is RE-OPENED. The gate for child-private data is GUARDIAN VERIFICATION,
orthogonal to payment. The good news: the platform ALREADY HAS the primitive —
claim_guardian_by_email() SECDEF + player_guardians(relationship,is_primary) +
parent_context_v (doc §2.6). The authority is the PROGRAM'S ROSTER (the program that
runs the team holds the guardian emails). So the corrected model:

  TIER 1 — PUBLIC TRACKING (free, open): subscribe to any scraped team to follow its
    PUBLIC data (schedule, scores, standings, odds) + TEAM-level push. No child
    identity. This is D-FV2 option (2): a family_subscriptions row, org-agnostic.
    Open claim is FINE here — it's following a team's already-public facts.
  TIER 2 — CHILD-PRIVATE (verified, then paid): adding a specific child's IDENTITY,
    uploading FILM of them, or per-CHILD targeting requires a VERIFIED GUARDIAN LINK
    via the existing claim_guardian authority — confirmed against the program's
    roster, NOT a credit card. Available ONLY for teams the platform has roster +
    guardian authority over (Legacy, St Patrick — programs we run). 

THE INVARIANT (write it into the entitlement code):
    canAccessChildData(user, child) = hasPaidEntitlement(user) AND
                                      isVerifiedGuardian(user, child)
  BOTH, independently. The paid check must NEVER imply guardianship; the guardian
  check must never be satisfiable by payment.

CROSS-PROGRAM (Q2): build the family_subscriptions spine org-agnostic (cheap future-
  proofing) so cross-program PUBLIC tracking "just works" later WITHOUT the full
  multi-org auth refactor (option 3) now. But note: for a team in a program we do
  NOT run, there is NO guardian-verification authority — so cross-program CHILD data
  stays unavailable (public-tracking-only) until a verification path exists. That's
  not a limitation to engineer around; it's the safety boundary.

C. FILM (D-FV7) — TRACK 2, AND GATED ON A REAL POSTURE (not just "later + private")
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Carving film off as a later, lowest-dependency track: ACCEPTED. "Private profile-
scoped bucket": necessary, NOT sufficient. Before a single frame is uploaded, ALL of:
  1. GUARDIAN GATE (§B): only a verified guardian may upload film tied to a child.
  2. THE MULTI-MINOR REALITY (CC didn't surface this): "game film" contains MANY
     minors — teammates AND opponents, none of whom consented to sit in a commercial
     cloud bucket processed by an AI sub-processor. Design for it: MINIMIZE raw
     retention (process → keep only the claimed child's clips → discard/expire the
     raw multi-child footage), no broadcast, hard deletion-on-demand.
  3. JERSEY-OCR ONLY, explicitly NO FACIAL RECOGNITION of minors. The feature is
     object/number detection (the jersey number), not biometric face-matching.
     Keep it that way — never add face recognition of children.
  4. SUB-PROCESSOR / CONSENT POSTURE: the AI clip service (Replicate or similar)
     becomes a sub-processor receiving video of minors — needs a DPA + an explicit
     guardian consent flow before upload. CC already flagged this; reinforcing it as
     a hard precondition, not a checklist item.
  5. RETENTION + DELETION: film must not outlive the relationship; a guardian can
     delete it; expiry is default-on.
Film does NOT proceed on "later" alone — it proceeds when §B (verification) is live
AND 1–5 are designed. Until then, not a line of upload code.
