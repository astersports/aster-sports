1. THE KIND-PICKER CUT — GROUNDED MANUAL vs AUTO-PROPOSED SPLIT
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The architect asked the audit to CONFIRM the exact split. Determined from the
ACTUAL cron handlers (supabase/functions/briefing-auto-draft-tick/_handlers.ts)
— this CORRECTS the assumed split:

  AUTO-PROPOSED (has a trigger handler -> arrives as a RADAR card; NOT in the
  manual picker):
    weekly_digest      handleWeeklySunday        (Sun 08:00 ET)
    schedule_change    handleScheduleChanged     (event_change_audit < 24h)
    game_recap         handleGameCompleted       (game_results published < 7d)
    tournament_prelim  handleTournamentApproaching (start in lead_time_hours)
    tournament_recap   handleTournamentCompleted (end_date < 14d)
    rsvp_nudge         handleRsvpLow24h          (coverage < threshold, <24h)

  MANUAL (no trigger -> admin starts by hand -> the "+ New" picker):
    announcement, custom_message, games_recap, coach_roundup, family_guide

  SPECIAL (not in the general picker):
    academy_callup_notice — EventDetail-initiated (wizardSupported:false,
      kindMetadata.js:145); opens the composer with the anchor pre-set.

  DELTAS vs the architect's assumption (worth noting):
    - coach_roundup: assumed auto -> actually MANUAL (no handler).
    - games_recap: assumed auto -> actually MANUAL (no handler).
    - rsvp_nudge: assumed token-only -> actually AUTO-PROPOSED (has a handler);
      it is also a token kind, but it reaches the admin as a Radar card.

  => THE CUT: the manual "+ New" picker shrinks from 12 flat tiles to FIVE
     (announcement, custom_message, games_recap, coach_roundup, family_guide).
     The 6 auto-proposed kinds leave the picker entirely (they live in Radar).
     academy_callup stays its own EventDetail flow. This is the single biggest
     simplification and it was the missed cut.

  Grouping for the 5 (useKindUsage ranks within):
    Messages:  announcement, custom_message
    Recaps:    games_recap
    Team-facing: coach_roundup, family_guide
  (At 5 kinds, a flat usage-ranked list may be enough; group only if it helps.)
