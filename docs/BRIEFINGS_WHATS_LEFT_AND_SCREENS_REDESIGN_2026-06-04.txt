PART A — THE SCREENS/OPTIONS REDESIGN (Frank's main concern)
==============================================================================

A0. THE CONCERN, STATED PLAINLY
The L99/Track-R redesign ADDED clean surfaces (Radar, a one-screen composer,
the coach view, the parent inbox) but never RETIRED or SIMPLIFIED the old
compose flow. The result is MORE fragmentation, not less: there are now TWO
composers living side by side, and the original messy one is untouched. The
stated purpose — "clean up the screens and the options" — did not ship.

A1. CURRENT SCREEN MAP (grounded in src/App.jsx)
  /admin/briefings            -> redirects to /radar
  /admin/briefings/radar      -> BriefingsRadarPage   (proposal cards + "+ New")
  /admin/briefings/new        -> BriefingNewPage       (R-2 ONE-SCREEN composer,
                                                        free-form; AI-1 lives here)
  /admin/briefings/compose    -> BriefingsComposePage  (the 4-STEP WIZARD,
                                                        BriefingComposer; AI-2 here)
  /admin/briefings/history    -> sent list + detail
  /inbox                      -> parent-facing inbox (#690)
  /team-briefings             -> coach read-only view (#707)

  => TWO composers: /new (one-screen) AND /compose (4-step wizard). Plus Radar.
     Three different "start a briefing" mental models.

A2. THE WIZARD STEP 1 IS OVERLOADED (IMG_2194–96 = /compose)
  "Compose · Kind", Step 1 of 4, stacks THREE jobs on one screen:
    (1) Send-now / Schedule-for-later radio (ScheduleForLaterPicker, shown on
        EVERY step via BriefingComposer).
    (2) "Resume a draft?" list (DraftResumeRow) — 5 in-progress drafts.
    (3) The kind grid (StepKindPicker) — ALL 12 kinds, 2-col tiles, each with
        icon + title + description + "Last sent Xd ago · N sent".
  Then a disabled "Next". You must scroll past send-mode + 5 drafts + 12 tiles
  before you've decided what you're doing.

A3. WHAT L99/TRACK-R DID vs MISSED
  DID (real wins, keep them):
    - Radar replaced the old briefings list (#704).
    - One-screen free-form compose (#706) — the RIGHT direction.
    - Coach read-only view (#707); parent inbox minimal (#690); RLS role-scoping
      (#710); audience labels (#703); a pile of engine bug fixes.
  MISSED (the core purpose):
    - Never unified the two composers. /new and /compose both exist.
    - Never simplified the kind picker (still all 12 kinds, flat, dense).
    - Never de-overloaded Step 1 (send-mode + resume + tiles all co-located).
    - "Resume a draft" duplicates Radar (the #690-vs-/inbox overlap pattern,
      now repeated inside the wizard).
    - 4 steps for what is often a 1-screen task.
  Net: the redesign added a clean surface NEXT TO the messy one instead of
  replacing it. That is the gap.

A4. THE SCREEN/OPTIONS PROBLEMS (the redesign target list)
  P1. Two composers -> one (cognitive split + double maintenance + drift risk).
  P2. Kind picker shows all 12 flat; no grouping, no progressive disclosure.
  P3. Step-1 overload (3 jobs on the kind screen).
  P4. Resume-draft surface duplicates Radar.
  P5. 4 steps; steps 1–2 (kind, anchor+audience) are often trivially derivable.
  P6. Options drift (audience picker per D-2; send-mode placement; test-only
      toggle placement) — scattered, inconsistent between /new and /compose.

A5. REDESIGN GOALS
  G1. ONE compose surface and one mental model.
  G2. Kind selection that is FAST: grouped + usage-ranked + progressive (don't
      show 12 equal tiles; surface the 3–4 the admin actually uses).
  G3. Each screen/step does ONE job. Send-mode and resume do NOT live on the
      kind picker.
  G4. No duplicate draft surfaces — drafts live in Radar (one place).
  G5. Options consolidated + identical wherever compose happens.

A6. PROPOSED DIRECTIONS (architect routes one)
  OPTION A (RECOMMENDED) — Collapse to one composer.
    Retire the 4-step wizard (/compose). Extend the one-screen composer (/new)
    to ALL kinds: the screen renders the kind's fields + the AI Draft control +
    inline preview + send. Kind selection becomes a lean entry ("What do you
    want to send?") — grouped + usage-ranked, no send-mode/resume clutter.
    Send-mode moves to the send action; resume moves to Radar.
    + Truest to "clean up the screens." One model. Kills P1–P5 at once.
    + The one-screen model + AI draft already work for free-form (AI-1) and the
      anchored body editors already exist (StepBodySignoff bodies) — this is a
      re-host, not a rebuild.
    - Biggest single change; needs a careful per-kind field audit + the
      schedule/test/audience options re-homed. §16.15 audit before PR A.

  OPTION B — Keep a wizard, radically simplify.
    Merge steps 1+2 (kind + anchor/audience derive together), group the kinds,
    move resume to Radar, move send-mode to the send step. Two composers remain.
    + Lower blast radius.
    - Leaves P1 (two composers) unsolved; half-measure again.

  OPTION C — Radar-first.
    Compose always starts from a Radar card (a proposal or a "+ New" intent);
    no standalone kind grid. The kind is chosen inside a single compose sheet.
    + Boldest simplification; one entry point (Radar).
    - Largest IA change; needs Radar to cover the free-form/manual case well.

  terminal-CC lean: OPTION A. It is the only one that actually delivers the
  stated purpose (one clean compose surface), and the pieces to build it already
  exist. B is another half-measure; C is a bigger IA bet than the problem needs.
