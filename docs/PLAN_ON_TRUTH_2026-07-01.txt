PART A — THE AAU PARENT EXPERIENCE (design plan)
=====================================================================

WHO THIS PARENT IS
Not a Tuesday-rec parent. AAU = logistics under stress, punctuated by pride: a
schedule that dropped Wednesday night, three pool games Saturday across two gyms,
a bracket seed unknown until other teams finish, a 7:40 tip = leave at 6:55, a
second kid on another team, a grandparent asking "did they win?" Lived in a loud
gym, one bar of signal, one hand, two-second glances. Judged in the first 30
seconds of the first Saturday on one question: did it make me feel calm and in
control, or behind?

THE NORTH-STAR PROMISE
By Sunday night, a parent never once had to wonder where to be, when to leave, or
whether their kid's team was still in it — and at least twice, the app gave them
a reason to feel proud. Calm as the floor. Pride as the ceiling.

FIVE PARENTS, ONE HOME, ZERO CONFIGURATION
The home self-assembles around THEIR family (tracked teams + their kids) — no
dashboards to build, no filters to set:
  • Logistics Parent (majority) — where/when/court/leave-by → the Up-Next hero.
  • Proud Parent — score/result/who's-next/clip → pride surfaces itself.
  • New/Anxious Parent — what happens, what to bring → first-run warmth.
  • Multi-Kid Parent — two teams, one brain → one merged agenda, never two apps.
  • Remote Family (grandparent/away) — follow along → live scores now, film later.
  • (cross-cutting) Spanish-speaking Parent — full EN↔ES parity, never second-class.
The win: none of them touches a setting to get their version.

DESIGN PRINCIPLES (gym-reality, explicit)
  1. Glanceable in 2s, one-handed. Next game + when-to-leave is the hero, always,
     above the fold, thumb-reachable. Everything else one tap deeper (hero +
     collapsible pattern, §16.14).
  2. Calm by default — never shouts unless it matters. Notifications auto-DRAFT
     for admin send; nothing floods the parent. Over-messaging loses AAU parents.
  3. Honest or silent — never confidently wrong. Exact where we have it;
     "Schedule releases Wednesday" over a fake time; no invented odds until
     calibrated. Silence beats fabrication. A design value, not just eng.
  4. Pride is engineered, not hoped for. Result / "in the bracket" / season arc /
     clip surfaced TO the parent, not buried in a stats page. Team-level +
     engagement only — never per-kid game stats (§16.12).
  5. Works on one bar of signal. Fast first paint, tiny payloads, cached
     last-known truth (the 52KB→3KB work is this principle).
  6. Progressive depth, never a paywall wall. Anxiety-killing logistics FREE
     forever (the trust deposit). Money buys MORE calm (leave-by, multi-game) and
     MORE pride (film) — never the basics.

SURFACE PLAN (tiered by trust)
FREE — "I always know where to be" (anxiety killer / trust deposit):
  • Up-Next Hero — next game · opponent · court · time · weather · one-tap
    directions. The whole app in one card (AauUpNextHero exists; make it the
    unmistakable center of gravity).
  • Self-assembling family agenda — one team or four, one kid or three, merged
    chronologically.
  • Live scores + honest bracket — Playoff Path before it's played, honest TBD
    for unresolved seeds, never a fabricated matchup.
  • Track-from-search, synced across devices — tap Track and it follows you on
    your account (the #1172 fix is a DESIGN requirement: losing a tracked team
    loses faith).
  • Findable from a Google search — the parent without the app yet types
    "[team] schedule" and lands on us (indexability = part of the experience).
PLUS — "I leave at the right minute, and the weekend just works" (paid calm):
  • Traffic-aware leave-by — real traffic, not distance÷speed. "Leave by 6:52" is
    the most valuable sentence you can hand an AAU parent at 6:40am.
  • Multi-game / multi-gym conflict solving — travel math before you're stuck.
  • Schedule-change alerts that feel like a heads-up from a friend — warm,
    actionable, only when it matters.
PREMIUM — "I got to see it, and so did Grandma" (shareable pride):
  • Family Film Room — clips, guardian-gated, jersey-not-face, one-tap delete.
    Last to build, strictest to protect.

PRIDE LAYER (Nike-Run-Club move, within the rules — team-level / engagement only)
  • "You're in the bracket" — quiet celebratory state change on advancement.
  • Weekend recap the parent didn't assemble — Sunday-night, warm, shareable.
  • Belonging cues — club identity (gold-on-navy, constellation mark).
  • Engagement streaks (attendance) — family-private, never a shaming leaderboard.

TRUST & SAFETY AS FELT EXPERIENCE
Kid privacy is visceral to an AAU parent. The design SHOWS the guardian gate,
never invents a stat/matchup, never floods the phone, makes film consent +
deletion one obvious tap. The parent should feel — without being told — that the
app treats their kid the way they would.

INCLUSION (table stakes, not a phase)
Full EN↔ES parity; VoiceOver; Dynamic Type; ≥4.5:1 contrast; 44px targets. The
grandparent on an old iPhone with big text still gets the Up-Next hero, clean.

SATISFACTION SIGNALS
  • First-Saturday retention (opened again next game day?).
  • Zero "where do we go?" texts in the team thread (logistics absorbed).
  • Track-rate (trust deposited).
  • Time-to-answer ~2s (open → "I know where to be").
  • Free→Plus conversion after a good first weekend.

The 48h lesson: the failure mode is live-contact bugs, not bad architecture.
  0.1 SMOKE THE DEP STORM across all five apps — one boot + core-flow pass per
      repo (Hub loads/tracks/searches; .io landing + Scout answers; @aster/weather
      resolves). Highest priority: widest unverified surface; everything
      downstream assumes these run. Green ≠ runtime.
  0.2 HARDEN THE R1·PR-A HUB TAIL — one deliberate real-device/real-data pass
      (multi-tournament clubs, bracket-TBD teams, forfeit rows, sign-in merge,
      password recovery end-to-end); close what surfaces. This IS the R1 "canary
      at parity" — treat it as a gate.
  Nothing competes with Phase 0.

PHASE 1 — FINISH R1 (free wedge) · ungated, highest funnel leverage
  • Indexability — serverless prerender (content is live, route set dynamic),
    smallest-blast-first: (1) real robots/sitemap + per-route
    title/meta/canonical/og carved ABOVE the catch-all rewrite; (2) crawlable
    <body> for shell + schedule; (3) standings-in-HTML waits on the PR-B freeze.
    Fetch ONLY anon/public RPCs (never behind the guardian gate — keeps the
    CHILD contract true from R1). No owner/schema/money action.
  • PR-B truth port — OWNER-GATED: apply the held standings/bracket substrate +
    freeze PUBLIC_RPC_CONTRACT.md, then port standings/odds to frozen RPCs and
    DELETE client-side math (where the live regressions die).

PHASE 2 — R2 FRONT-DOOR CUTOVER · on Vercel, reversible
  Cut over once, early, on a proven Hub, BEFORE money. Soft pointer already
  staged. Hard 301s wait on: G1 anon-tracking cross-origin handoff (localStorage
  origin-scoped — nudge sign-in or serialize keys through the redirect); G2
  route-level 301 map (legacy has real schedule/records routes); G3
  index-at-parity. Retire the legacy Hub + the duplicated forfeit engine here.

PHASE 3 — R3 HUB PLUS MONEY-PATH · port the Stripe template
  ONE entitlement-writer service, TWO signed sources (Stripe + Apple ASN), client
  never writes, super-user OR's into the CHECK. Traffic vendor (TomTom/Google
  Routes) LOCKED before build — leave-by is the paid headline. [VERIFY] Apple
  IAP/external-link policy at design time. Test-mode-first, owner-applied; alert
  on any non-writer touching entitlements.

PHASE 4–6
  R4 completeness — push + multi-game/multi-gym travel conflicts + weekend agenda;
    odds un-suppress only after calibration (Brier earned).
  R5 Program B2B — real RBAC lands BEFORE B2B; charging serialized behind a proven

  R6 Premium/Film — family film room, jersey-not-face, canAccessChild false even

  PARALLEL — Apple: start Developer Program enrollment NOW (long-lead D-U-N-S +
