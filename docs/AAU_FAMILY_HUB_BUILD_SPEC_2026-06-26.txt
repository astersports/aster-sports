PART 1 — THE PRODUCT (what we're building, in full)
================================================================================
A public, FAMILY-CENTRIC basketball hub. The organizing primitive is the FAMILY,
not the org or the team. Marketed as: "one account, every kid, every game,
everywhere they play."

The parent journey:
  1. TRACK (free, public): a parent opens the AAU hub and follows any team that the
     tournament scrape already discovered — their kid's team(s), a rival, a whole
     division. They get that team's PUBLIC data: schedule, live scores, division
     standings, bracket + AI-narrated odds, and push when a score/schedule/final
     lands. No login required to view; a free account to follow + get push. NO child
     identity anywhere on this surface.
  2. CLAIM (verified, paid): to add THEIR kid — pin a specific child, get per-child
     notifications, and (later) upload film — the parent must be a VERIFIED GUARDIAN
     (confirmed against the program roster) AND hold a paid family entitlement. The
     two are independent gates; neither substitutes for the other.
  3. FILM (later track): a verified guardian uploads their own game film; AI clips
     highlights to their child's jersey number. Held behind a full minors'-privacy
     posture.

The flexibility the operator asked for: pick 5th-grade-girls + 8th-grade-boys; or
compare 5th-grade-girls A vs B vs C divisions side by side. Simple but flexible.

The standings/predictor the operator asked for: show the division standings (with
the circuit's tiebreaker rules applied — e.g. Zero Gravity caps any win at +20,
2-way ties = head-to-head, 3-way = point differential), show who's in 1st/2nd, who
advances to the bracket, where/when the bracket games are, and an AI predictor of
the odds of making the bracket — with the math exact and only the wording AI.

PART 2 — ARCHITECTURE & DATA TOPOLOGY
================================================================================
WHERE IT LIVES [RATIFIED D-FV1(b)]: a PUBLIC, family-scoped view IN the aster-sports
  app (where the Supabase data + ~90% of the reusable UX already live), served by
  anon-safe SECDEF public RPCs (the get_public_* pattern legacy-hoopers already
  ships). astersports.io's AAU page LINKS into it. The data layer is NOT
  re-implemented in the astersports-web MySQL/Drizzle stack.

SINGLE SOURCE OF TRUTH: the aster-sports Supabase project (vrwwpsbfbnveawqwbdmj).

DATA CLASSIFICATION [RATIFIED D-FV8]:
  PUBLIC (anon SECDEF RPC, PII-stripped): the tournament's factual state — schedule,
    scores, division standings, bracket, AI odds.
  CONTAINED (auth + verified guardian + paid): a specific child's identity, per-child
    notifications, uploaded film. Visible only inside the verified family profile.

THE CHILD-SAFETY INVARIANT [RATIFIED, load-bearing]:
    canAccessChildData(user, child) = hasPaidEntitlement(user)
                                      AND isVerifiedGuardian(user, child)
  evaluated LIVE at read time. Guardianship is DURABLE (the link persists, FK + trigger
  enforced); entitlement is CURRENT (a lapse suspends access while the link remains).
  A paywall is NOT a child-safety gate — verified guardianship is. The two are
  physically separate columns; neither is satisfiable by the other.

THREE BUILD TRACKS:
  TRACK 0  Standings spine (non-money, non-child-data): division/pool/bracket schema +
           the rule-driven standings engine + the public standings RPC. Unblocks the
           demoable public v1.
  TRACK 1  Family hub + live data: the public family view, the family-claim/subscription
           model, the bracket predictor + AI odds, subscriber push.
  TRACK 2  Film (parallel, last): parent upload + AI jersey clips. Held for the privacy
           posture (§C).

PART 4 — SCHEMA (full inventory; drafts in PR #1103, NOT applied)
================================================================================
All schema is a design-review artifact in docs/; owner applies after this review
(moves to supabase/migrations with the prod version prefix per AP #21). RLS uses the
existing helpers (current_user_org_id, user_has_role_in_org); SECDEF gate helpers

with WITH CHECK on writes (AP #20).

--- TRACK 0: STANDINGS SUBSTRATE (docs/TRACK0_STANDINGS_SCHEMA_DRAFT_2026-06-26.sql)
  tournament_divisions      a division within one tournament. advance_count (top-N to
                            bracket), circuit (-> circuit_rules tiebreakers), grade/gender.
  tournament_division_teams THE STANDINGS UNIT. team_id XOR opponent_id XOR scraped name;
                            generated resolved_key = id-or-normalized-name; UNIQUE per
                            division so a team can't split its standings; promotion-safe.
  tournament_pools          a pool within a division ("Pool A").
  pool_teams                membership of a standings unit in a pool.
  bracket_slots             playoff structure: round, slot_index, seed_source,
                            division_team_id (fills as it resolves), event_id (the
                            scheduled bracket game = where/when), advances_to_slot_id.
  events.tournament_division_id / tournament_pool_id   nullable; scopes OUR games.
  [OPEN OQ6] division_games  the games SOURCE for standings — see Part 8. The engine
                            already consumes a generic games array; this is purely where
                            the full division board (incl. opponent-vs-opponent) lands.

--- TRACK 1: FAMILY TRUST (docs/TRACK1_FAMILY_TRUST_SCHEMA_DRAFT_2026-06-26.sql)
  family_subscriptions  TIER 1. user_id + 3-way team identity, org-agnostic, open follow.
                        notify_schedule/score/final. NO child column at all -> following a
                        team is structurally incapable of leaking child data.
  family_children       TIER 2. user_id + player_guardian_id (FK to the verified link).
                        BEFORE trigger asserts the linked guardian's user_id = this user
                        (email-confirmed claim) -> un-creatable without verified guardianship,
                        enforced for EVERY writer incl. service_role. Guardianship durable.
  family_entitlements   D-FV9. Stripe-derived (status active/trialing/past_due/canceled/
                        inactive + current_period_end + stripe ids), UNIQUE(user_id),
                        webhook-written only (no authenticated write policy).
  is_entitled(user)                live entitlement check, caller-scoped.
  can_access_child(user, player)   is_entitled AND verified-guardian-re-checked-LIVE;
                        caller-scoped; the ONLY place the two gates combine; the film/
                        per-child gate.

PART 8 — OPEN DECISION: OQ6 (division games source)
================================================================================
(Full detail in docs/OQ6_DIVISION_GAMES_DECISION_2026-06-26.txt.)
THE FACT: opponent-vs-opponent games have NO events row (events.team_id = our teams
  only) and no game_results row (our-team-centric). So "query events by
  tournament_division_id" yields CORRECT-BUT-PARTIAL standings (our games only) — every
  external team shows only its games against us. The scraper already pulls the whole
  division board; it just has nowhere to land.
OPTIONS: A) division_games table (one games source per division, scraper writes the whole
  board; our scores stay authoritative in game_results and mirror in on publish) —
  CC LEAN. B) events-only / accept partial standings. C) B now, A later.
NEEDED: rule A / B / C. A is required for true division standings + the predictor (the
  operator's headline ask); B/C are legitimate v1-scope calls IF the partial-standings
  limit is a labeled, deliberate choice.
