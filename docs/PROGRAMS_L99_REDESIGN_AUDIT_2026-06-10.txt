§2. (b) DEEP-READ ADDENDUM — what the first pass under-weighted
==============================================================================
D-1 [P1] THREE-WAY "program" vocabulary (programs-archetype / season-view /
   teams-via-usePrograms). The redesign's simplification win is as much
   NOMENCLATURE as UI. Any wireframe that says "Program" must pin which of the
   three it means on every surface. This is the highest-leverage simplification
   and it's invisible in a per-file pass — it only appears across files.

D-2 [P1] The PARENT has no programs surface AT ALL, yet "100% for all three
   roles" is the goal. The parent's entire relationship to "programs" today is:
   (1) a pre-account anon funnel they may never see again, and (2) registration/
   payment status that lives in Financials + registration.status, surfaced
   NOWHERE to the parent. The biggest redesign question (RQ1) is a BUILD question
   for parent, not a polish question: there is no surface to polish.

D-3 [P2] The COACH has no programs surface either, but unlike parent, a coach's
   "program" need is already partly met elsewhere (their teams via team-scoped
   roster/schedule). The RQ1 coach question is "is there a program-level view a
   coach needs that team-level views don't already give?" — CC lean: minimal;
   coaches think in teams, not programs. Surface evidence, architect decides.

D-4 [P2] The registration FUNNEL and the registration ADMIN view are two halves
   of one contract that don't talk: the anon funnel writes registrations the
   admin detail page lists, but neither shows the server-authoritative money, and
   the parent who submitted sees a "Spot reserved!" then nothing. RD-4
   "reconciliation contract half-built" (MASTER §0) is literal and spans 3
   surfaces (confirm screen, admin detail, parent post-account).

D-5 [P2] divisions=0 org-wide. Every division/fee surface (DivisionRows public
   DivisionCard, estimateCart, submit_registration fee insert) is structurally
   built and ZERO-exercised. The redesign should treat the create→register→bill
   money path as effectively greenfield-with-a-skeleton, not a working surface to
   refine. (Behavioral hole §8.)

D-6 [P3] AdminSeasonsPage's status-on-create (active|archived by seasons.length)
   bypasses the registry's draft-on-create. Two create doors, two status models.
   Collapsing AdminSeasonsPage INTO the programs surface (RQ4) also closes this.

D-7 [P3] There is no "Archive" action anywhere in the UI, yet "Archive it
   instead" is the delete guard's recommended path. The recommended safe action
   is unreachable. Small but a real 10/10 gap.
